Receptor coverage
Every major innate immune sensor
TLR1/2 · TLR2/6 · TLR4 — ML model
The three most drug-targeted TLRs. Binding predictions use a trained XGBoost model (R²=0.71) fit on experimental pIC50 data from published lipopeptide and LPS analogue studies. Outputs include estimated IC50 in nM, pharmacophore contact scores for key residues (Asn294, Phe349, Glu271), and lipid anchor detection. The model takes 2D molecular descriptors: MW, LogP, acyl chain count, and fingerprint-derived features.
TLR3 · TLR7 · TLR8 · TLR9 — nucleic acid sensors
Endosomal TLRs that detect nucleic acid patterns from pathogens. TLR3 recognises double-stranded RNA (poly I:C analogues; PDB 3CIY); TLR7 and TLR8 sense single-stranded RNA and small-molecule agonists such as imiquimod and resiquimod (PDB 5GMH, 3W3J); TLR9 detects unmethylated CpG ODNs from bacteria and viruses (PDB 3WPB). Each analysis is grounded in the corresponding crystal structure.
STING · cGAS — cytosolic DNA sensing
STING (Stimulator of Interferon Genes) and its upstream sensor cGAS detect foreign or misplaced cytosolic DNA — a hallmark of viral infection, cancer mutation load, or cellular stress. STING agonists (cyclic dinucleotides, DMXAA analogues; PDB 4LOH) are among the most active areas in cancer immunotherapy, with multiple compounds in Phase II/III trials. cGAS inhibitors are being explored in autoimmune disease.
RIG-I · MDA5 — cytosolic RNA sensing
DExD/H-box RNA helicases that sense double-stranded RNA generated during viral replication. RIG-I preferentially recognises short 5′-triphosphate dsRNA (PDB 4A36); MDA5 forms cooperative filaments on long dsRNA (PDB 4GL2). Both activate the MAVS signalling axis, triggering type-I interferon production. Active drug programmes target influenza, SARS-CoV-2, and RSV using synthetic RIG-I / MDA5 agonists.
Under the hood
How the predictions are made
ML model — TLR1/2, TLR2/6, TLR4
XGBoost · R²=0.71
Trained on experimental pIC50 measurements from published lipopeptide binding studies. Features include molecular weight, cLogP, rotatable bond count, acyl chain count, hydrogen bond donors and acceptors, and 2048-bit Morgan fingerprint-derived descriptors. The model outputs a pIC50 value directly; IC50 is back-calculated as 10^(9−pIC50) nM. Confidence is highest for lipopeptide scaffolds structurally similar to training data (Pam3CSK4, FSL-1, lipid A analogues).
LLM analysis — all other receptors
Claude Sonnet · Expert context
Each of the 14 receptors has a hand-curated pharmacophore context: key binding residues from X-ray crystallography (PDB), known agonist and antagonist scaffolds with their potency ranges, and selectivity-determining structural features. The LLM evaluates the input SMILES against this expert context and returns a structured binding score (0–100), pharmacophore match score, key contact flags, drug-likeness assessment, and a free-text expert narrative explaining the rationale.
Use cases
Built for researchers who move fast
💉
Vaccine adjuvant design
Screen synthetic lipopeptide and TLR agonist libraries before committing to SPPS or total synthesis. Rapidly distinguish TLR1/2 vs TLR2/6 selectivity — the two heterodimers have distinct binding pocket geometries and acyl chain preferences that ImmunoBind resolves in a single screen.
🧬
Cancer immunotherapy
Evaluate STING agonists, TLR7/8 agonists, and RIG-I activators for tumour microenvironment reprogramming. Screen cyclic dinucleotide analogues, DMXAA derivatives, and imidazoquinoline scaffolds against the appropriate receptor before moving into cell-based NF-κB or IFN-β reporter assays.
🦖
Antiviral drug discovery
Identify compounds that activate innate antiviral responses via RIG-I, MDA5, TLR3, TLR7, or TLR9. Useful for screening candidates that trigger interferon responses as part of a combination antiviral strategy, or for identifying off-target TLR activation in drug safety profiling.
