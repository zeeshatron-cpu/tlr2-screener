Receptor coverage
Every major innate immune sensor
Toll-like receptors (TLR1–10)
The primary bacterial and viral sensors on immune cells. TLR1/2 and TLR2/6 detect lipopeptides; TLR4 detects LPS; TLR3/7/8/9 detect viral nucleic acids. Heavily targeted for vaccine adjuvants and sepsis drugs.
STING & cGAS
STING and its upstream sensor cGAS detect foreign or misplaced DNA — a sign of viral infection or cancer mutation. STING agonists are one of the most active areas in cancer immunotherapy, with multiple compounds in clinical trials.
RIG-I & MDA5
Intracellular sensors that detect double-stranded RNA produced during viral replication. Activating them triggers a strong antiviral interferon response. Drug candidates are being explored for influenza, COVID-19, and RSV.
ML + LLM analysis
TLR1/2, TLR2/6, and TLR4 use a trained XGBoost model (R²=0.71) for pIC50 prediction. All other receptors use expert LLM analysis with receptor-specific pharmacophore context and PDB reference structures.
